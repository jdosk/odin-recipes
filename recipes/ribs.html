Smoked & Braised Short Ribs

Smoked and braised short ribs over mashed potatoes

This dish is one of my favorites. It combines the subtle flavors of smoked ribs with the depth and intensity of a long braise in red wine sauce. The meat ends up melt in your mouth tender and exremely rich in flavor.

  • 8-12 Bone in short ribs
  • 1/2 Bottle of red wine, preferably Cabernet or burgandy
  • 1 White or yellow onion
  • 2 Large carrots
  • 1 Tbs. of tomato paste
  • 1 Large can of beef or chicken stock
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 3 cloves of garlic, chopped
  • 1 sprig of chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig of chopped fresh thyme
  • Kosher salt
  • Pepper
  1. Preheat smoker to 225 degrees.
  2. Liberally season short ribs with salt and pepper.
  3. Place short ribs on smoker and smoke for 1 1/2 to 2 hours. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  4. While ribs smoke, chop onion and carrots into large chunks. Place into deep cooking pan, along with chpped garlic, rosemary, thyme, bay leaf, and tomato paste. Pour red wine and beef stock into pan and mix thouroughly. heat pan in oven for last 15 minutes before pulling short ribs off the smoker.
  5. Pull short ribs off smoker and place bone side down into cooking pan. The meat should be partially covered with liquid, add more stock if needed. Cover pan with aluminum foil and place into oven (or smoker, just raise tempature to 350).
  6. Cook meat for another 2 hours. After 2 hours, peel back foil and poke ribs with a fork. If fork slides in effortlessly, the ribs are done. If not, cover and place back into oven. Cook another 30 minutes and retest. Repeat until ribs are tender.
  7. When ribs are finished, remove from pan and cover them with foil. Strain the cooking liquid into pot. Let cool for 5 minutes and use a spoon to scoop oil off the top. Bring sauce to simmer, and cook for 5 minutes. If desired, quickly whisk a pinch of flour to thicken.
  8. Serve meat over mashed potatoes, and serve with sauce as a jus.
